Designing a Safe Website

Learning Intentions

  • Understand what makes a website safe and trustworthy.
  • Identify security features and risks.
  • Design a mock-up of a secure website homepage.

Part 1: Understanding Website Safety (10 minutes)

Read the passage carefully:

Every website collects some kind of data — your name, password, or payment details.
A safe website keeps that data protected through encryption, secure design, and honesty.

Three key things to look for on a safe website:

  1. HTTPS in the web address — it means the connection is secure.
  2. Padlock icon in the address bar.
  3. Privacy or consent notice explaining how data is used.

Unsafe websites might have strange URLs, grammar mistakes, fake pop-ups, or ask for personal details they don’t need.

Part 2: Cyber Threats Awareness (10 minutes)

Read:

Threat What It Means Example
Phishing Fake websites or emails trick people into giving passwords. A fake “bank login” page.
Malware Software that harms your computer or steals data. Clicking a link that installs a virus.
Weak Passwords Easy to guess, allowing hackers access. “12345” or “password”.
